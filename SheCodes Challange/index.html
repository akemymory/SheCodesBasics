CrossFit is a high-intensity fitness training program that combines elements from various disciplines such as cardiovascular conditioning, weightlifting, gymnastics, and functional movements. It was created by Greg Glassman in 2000 and has gained significant popularity as a training methodology and sport.

CrossFit workouts can include activities like weightlifting, running, rowing, bodyweight exercises, kettlebell training, and more. They are typically performed at a high intensity and are designed to challenge multiple muscle groups and energy systems in a single session. CrossFit places a strong emphasis on functional movements, which are exercises that mimic real-life activities and help improve daily life activities and overall athleticism.

CrossFit also has a competitive aspect with events like the CrossFit Games, which is an annual competition to find the "Fittest on Earth." Athletes from around the world compete in a variety of challenging workouts to showcase their strength, endurance, and overall fitness level.
